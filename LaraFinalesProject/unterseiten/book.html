A short summary of Bram Stoker's novel "Dracula"

"Dracula" is a classic gothic horror novel that tells the story of Count Dracula, a vampire from Transylvania, and his attempt to move to England in search of new blood. The novel is written in an epistolary format, with the narrative unfolding through a series of letters, diary entries, newspaper clippings, and journal entries.
The novel's central characters include Jonathan Harker, Mina Harker (née Murray), Professor Abraham Van Helsing, Dr. John Seward, Lucy Westenra, and Renfield. They become entangled in a battle against Count Dracula, who can manipulate the minds of those he preys upon.

As the group works to stop Dracula's reign of terror, the story explores themes of vampirism, sexuality, and the struggle between good and evil. It is a quintessential work of vampire literature that has had a profound influence on the vampire genre in literature and popular culture.
"Dracula" has been adapted into numerous films, television shows, and other media, and Count Dracula remains one of the most iconic and enduring figures in the horror genre.
